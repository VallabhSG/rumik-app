# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People who care deeply about audio quality — fidelity, mastering, dynamic range. They listen with good headphones, often late at night, in low ambient light. They're not passive consumers: they seek out specific artists, notice production details, and have strong opinions about what sounds right. Music is a primary experience for them, not background noise.

## Product Purpose

rumik is a music discovery and listening app for quality-first listeners. It exists to surface the kind of music audiophiles seek — well-produced, often electronic and synth-forward — and to do so with an interface that respects the listening experience rather than competing with it.

Success looks like: a user opens rumik and immediately feels like they're in the right place before a single track plays.

## Brand Personality

Warm, Restless, Nocturnal.

- **Warm**: Not cold or clinical. Analog texture in a digital shell. The darkness has weight, not emptiness.
- **Restless**: Always moving. Discovery is foregrounded. The UI rewards exploration without demanding it.
- **Nocturnal**: Built for low light and headphones. Every design decision should pass the "at midnight, eyes adjusted to dark" test.

## Anti-references

- Spotify: aggressively green, pop-maximalist, optimized for the lowest common denominator. rumik is the opposite of mass-market.
- Generic streaming dark mode: the cold, IDE-like dark that mistakes low chroma for sophistication. rumik's dark is warm, not grey.
- Hero-metric dashboards: big numbers, gradient accents, SaaS cliche. No.
- Identical card grids: same-sized cards repeated endlessly. Discovery should feel curated, not catalogued.

## Design Principles

1. **Audio is the hero.** The interface serves the music. Never compete visually with what's playing. Restraint is not timidity — it is respect.
2. **Warm dark, not cold dark.** The near-black backgrounds should have hue — toward amber or violet, not grey. Low light should feel like a listening room, not a data center.
3. **Quality signals in every detail.** Audiophiles notice things. Micro-spacing, type weight choices, subtle texture. The craft of the UI signals the craft of the music.
4. **Discovery that invites, not overwhelms.** Restlessness means forward momentum, not chaos. New content surfaces naturally; navigation never requires thought.
5. **Nothing earns its place twice.** Every label, every section header, every UI element should exist because removing it would cost something. Ruthless editing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Given the dark theme, particular attention to contrast ratios for body text and secondary labels against dark backgrounds. Reduced-motion alternatives for any ambient animation.
